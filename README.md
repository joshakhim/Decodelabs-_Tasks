# End-to-End E-Commerce Data Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview

This project demonstrates a complete end-to-end data analytics workflow using Python, Pandas, SQL, and exploratory data analysis techniques to analyze e-commerce transactional data.

The objective of the project was to simulate real-world business analysis processes by transforming raw transactional data into meaningful business insights through:

* Data Cleaning
* Data Validation
* Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* SQL Business Intelligence Reporting
* Revenue & Customer Analysis
* Business Insight Generation

The project was designed to replicate tasks commonly performed by junior data analysts in real business environments.

# 📊 Key Business Questions Answered

### Data Cleaning & Validation

* Are there missing or inconsistent values in the dataset?
* Are financial calculations accurate?
* Does the dataset contain duplicate transactions?
* Are date formats standardized and analysis-ready?

### Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

* Which products generate the highest revenue?
* Which payment methods are most frequently used?
* Which referral sources generate the most sales?
* How does revenue fluctuate over time?
* What customer purchasing patterns exist?

### SQL Business Analysis

* Which products contribute most to company revenue?
* Who are the highest-value customers?
* Which marketing channels perform best?
* What are the top-selling products by quantity?
* How is revenue distributed across order statuses?

---

# 📈 Key Insights

* Chairs generated the highest product revenue at **$195,620.11**
* Instagram produced the highest referral revenue at **$275,285.45**
* Online payments were the most frequently used transaction method
* Revenue fluctuated significantly across different months, suggesting seasonal purchasing behavior
* A small group of customers contributed disproportionately to overall revenue
